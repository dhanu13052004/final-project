Frequently Asked
Questions

Unlock the answers to your queries with our Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)!

  • What is the food like in New Zealand?

    • Wide variety of international cuisines including Indian, Asian, and European options • Indian groceries and spices available in major cities such as Auckland and Wellington • Universities provide diverse dining options including vegetarian and halal meals • Local markets and food festivals offer fresh and multicultural food experiences

  • Travel & Transportation

    • Student visa required for full-time study, with applications made through Immigration New Zealand • Direct flights connect major Indian cities with Auckland, Christchurch, and Wellington airports • Reliable public transport includes buses, trains, and ferries with student concession fares available • Compact cities allow easy commuting between home, campus, and recreational areas

  • What is included in the housing facilities?

    • University halls of residence offer secure, convenient accommodation • Off-campus housing options include shared flats, apartments, and homestays • Many students share rentals to reduce living expenses • Rental agreements require deposits and awareness of tenant rights

  • What languages do people use in New Zealand?

    • English is the language of instruction and day-to-day communication • Universities offer language support programs to help non-native speakers • Multicultural student communities encourage cultural exchange and language practice

  • Why Choose New Zealand for Higher Education?

    • Globally recognized universities with emphasis on research, innovation, and practical skills • Affordable tuition fees and living costs compared to other Western countries • Strong work-study opportunities with up to 25 hours per week allowed during studies (from Nov 2025) • Pathways to post-study work visas enabling graduates to gain work experience and residency • Safe, clean, and welcoming environment with high quality of life

  • How does the education system function in New Zealand?

    • Includes universities, polytechnics, and colleges offering undergraduate to doctoral programs • Education geared towards practical, hands-on learning with a strong research focus • Degrees internationally recognized and valued by employers worldwide • Academic calendar and admission processes well-structured for international students

  • What is the climate like in New Zealand?

    • Temperate climate with mild summers and cool, wet winters • Regional variations include cooler southern parts and warmer northern regions • Students should be prepared for variable weather and occasional rain

  • Stay Back & Future Opportunities

    • Students can work up to 25 hours per week during study periods (increased from 20 hours from Nov 2025) • Post-study work visas available for 1-3 years depending on qualification level • Strong job market in technology, agriculture, health, and engineering sectors • IIEC provides support with career planning, work permits, and immigration pathways

  • What is the climate like in New Zealand Currency?

    • New Zealand Dollar (NZD) is the official currency • As of October 2025, 1 Indian Rupee (INR) ≈ 0.022 NZD • Budgeting in NZD essential for managing tuition fees and living expenses

  • How would you describe the Culture of New Zealand?

    • Multicultural society emphasizing Maori, Pacific Islander, and Western traditions • Friendly, safe, and inclusive communities with vibrant student activities • Universities offer various cultural clubs, events, and support services

  • How is the Survivability in New Zealand?

    • High living standards with accessible healthcare and social services • Universities provide orientation, counseling, and ongoing student support • Safe cities and well-developed infrastructure facilitate student well-being • Strong peer and community networks help international students settle in
