Frequently Asked
Questions

Unlock the answers to your queries with our Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)!

  • What is the food like in Malaysia?

    • Wide variety of Malaysian, Indian, Chinese, and international cuisines • Indian groceries and spices available in urban areas and ethnic supermarkets • Universities provide vegetarian, halal, and diverse dietary options • Food courts and street food markets offer affordable, flavorful meals

  • Travel & Transportation

    • Student Pass required for full-time study, issued via Education Malaysia Global Services (EMGS) • Direct flights from India to Kuala Lumpur International Airport and other regional airports • Extensive public transport options include buses, light rail (LRT), monorail, and taxis • Affordable and efficient transport with student concession cards available • Well-connected road and rail networks facilitate travel within Malaysia

  • What is included in the housing facilities?

    • On-campus dormitories are common, providing secure, affordable accommodation • Off-campus housing options include apartments, flats, and shared rentals • Many students share accommodations to reduce costs • Homestays provide cultural immersion but are less prevalent • Rental agreements typically require deposits and minimum lease terms

  • What languages do people use in Malaysia?

    • Malay (Bahasa Malaysia) is the official language; English widely used in education and business • Universities offer courses primarily in English, especially at higher education levels • Language support services and cultural orientation programs available for international students • Multicultural campus environment supports cultural exchange and inclusion

  • Why Choose Malaysia for Higher Education?

    • Affordable tuition fees and cost of living compared to Western countries • High-quality education with many internationally accredited universities • Multicultural society offering rich cultural diversity and welcoming environment • Strategic location in Southeast Asia with growing economic and industrial sectors • Scholarships and financial aid widely accessible for international students

  • How does the education system function in Malaysia?

    • Comprises universities, technical colleges, polytechnics, and private institutions • Offers diploma, undergraduate, postgraduate, and doctoral programs • Curriculum combines academic theory with practical skills development • Degrees recognized globally with emphasis on employability and industry needs

  • What is the climate like in Malaysia?

    • Tropical climate with hot, humid weather year-round • Two main monsoon seasons bringing heavy rains: May-September and November-March • Temperatures generally range between 77°F (25°C) and 95°F (35°C) • Students should prepare for humidity and occasional heavy rainfall

  • Stay Back & Future Opportunities

    • Student Pass allows part-time work up to 20 hours per week during semester • Post-study work permits available, facilitating employment in Malaysia’s growing job market • Opportunities exist in sectors like technology, manufacturing, and services • IIEC provides

  • What is the climate like in Malaysia Currency?

    • Official currency is the Malaysian Ringgit (MYR) • As of October 2025, 1 Indian Rupee (INR) ≈ 0.057 MYR • Budgeting in MYR is important for managing tuition and living expenses

  • How would you describe the Culture of Malaysia?

    • Rich cultural blend mainly of Malay, Chinese, Indian, and indigenous traditions • Festivals, cuisine, and arts celebrate multiculturalism and community spirit • Emphasis on respect, hospitality, and family values • Universities promote vibrant student life with cultural clubs and events

  • How is the Survivability in Malaysia?

    • Malaysia offers a safe environment with modern healthcare and public services • Universities provide orientation, counseling, and student welfare programs • Affordable cost of living with access to modern infrastructure • Strong community and support networks help international students adjust
