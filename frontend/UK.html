Frequently Asked
Questions

Unlock the answers to your queries with our Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)!

  • What is the food like in UK?

    • Wide variety of international cuisines including Indian, Middle Eastern, and Western options • Numerous Indian grocery stores and restaurants in major cities with large Indian communities • Universities offer vegetarian, vegan, halal, and other special dietary options • Diverse food markets and cultural festivals available for eclectic tastes

  • Travel & Transportation

    • Student visa (formerly Tier 4, now Student route visa) required for full-time study • Direct flights connect major Indian cities to London Heathrow, Manchester, and other UK airports • Extensive public transport includes buses, trains, underground (Tube), and taxis • Student discount cards available for transport and other services • Rail and coach networks provide easy regional and national access.

  • What is included in the housing facilities?

    • Accommodation options include university halls of residence, private rentals, and shared housing • On-campus housing offers convenience, safety, and support services • Private rentals vary by city; early booking advised due to demand • Homestays are available but less common than in some countries • Rental contracts may require deposits and understanding of tenant rights

  • What languages do people use in UK?

    • English is the primary language of instruction and daily communication • Universities provide English language support including academic writing, speaking workshops, and tutoring • Diverse, multicultural student communities enhance language practice and cultural exchange

  • Why Choose UK for Higher Education?

    • Home to some of the world’s oldest and most prestigious universities • Shorter course durations (3 years for undergraduate, 1 year for master’s) compared to other countries • Strong global reputation for research, innovation, and academic excellence • Extensive scholarship opportunities for international students • Post-study work visa options available to gain valuable UK work experience • Rich cultural heritage and vibrant student life

  • How does the education system function in UK?

    • Includes undergraduate, postgraduate, and doctoral degree programs

  • What is the climate like in UK?

    • Temperate maritime climate with mild summers and cool, wet winters • Rainfall distributed throughout the year; occasional snow in winter months • Students should be prepared with appropriate clothing for changeable weather

  • Stay Back & Future Opportunities

    • Graduate Route visa allows students to work or look for work in the UK for up to 2 years post-study (3 years for PhDs) • Work experience gained improves prospects for skilled work visas and residency pathways • IIEC offers guidance on career planning, visa transitions, and UK employment market

  • What is the climate like in UK Currency?

    • Official currency is the British Pound Sterling (GBP) • As of October 2025, 1 Indian Rupee (INR) ≈ 0.010 GBP • Managing costs and budgeting in GBP is crucial for living and study expenses

  • How would you describe the Culture of UK?

    • Diverse and inclusive society with welcoming universities • Rich traditions in arts, history, and multicultural festivals • Active student unions and clubs promote socializing and personal development • Emphasis on independent thinking, debate, and community engagement

  • How is the Survivability in UK?

    • High standards of healthcare through the National Health Service (NHS) • Universities provide extensive support services including counseling, orientation, and academic advising • Safe cities with comprehensive student safety measures • Well-developed public infrastructure and community support networks for a smooth transition.
