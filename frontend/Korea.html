Why Study in Korea?

16000+ students successfully and 2500+ immigrants sent

The Republic of Korea is a highly successful country on the Asian continent. Most of the technology-related supply to India comes from South Korea. It is a global leader in technology and industrial sectors and provides a wide range of job opportunities in the same. South Korea is a place known for its impeccable education system; Edu-buffs are a thing here.

Did you know that Korea is among the top countries for a citizen’s basic level of education to be a tertiary degree? Great schools and up-date-curriculum account to the fact that nearly half of the educational institutions in Korea holds a high rate of foreign students. Many students who study in Korea are international students. Being among the prime players in the league, Korea’s GDP per capita is 29, 989; it is a high economy country with a high value of HDI. Korea is among the top countries to hold a place in the Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development (OECD) and performing gloriously in the fields of Math, Science, and Reading literary.

Frequently Asked
Questions

Unlock the answers to your queries with our Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)!

  • What is the food like in South Korea?

    • Wide availability of Korean cuisine on campus and local markets • Access to Indian groceries and spices in major cities like Seoul via ethnic supermarkets • Universities provide diverse dining options including vegetarian and halal meals • Increasing presence of international food outlets to cater to global tastes

  • Travel & Transportation

    • Student visa (D-2) required for programs longer than 90 days • Essential travel documents: passport, visa, Certificate of Admission from Korean university • Direct flights available from India to Seoul Incheon and other major airports • Extensive public transportation including subways, buses, and taxis with easy access to university areas • Affordable student transport cards are widely available for discounted travel

  • What is included in the housing facilities?

    • University dormitories provide convenient and affordable accommodation with diverse facilities • Off-campus housing options include apartments and guesthouses near campuses • Homestays offer cultural immersion and support for international students • Rental agreements usually require a deposit and minimum lease duration knowledge

  • What languages do people use in South Korea?

    • Korean is the primary language but increasing courses and support are available in English • Universities offer Korean language learning programs to help international students adapt • English is widely spoken in academic settings, especially in international programs • Opportunities for cultural exchange and language clubs enrich the student experience

  • Why Choose South Korea for Higher Education?

    • Rapidly growing global reputation for advanced research and technology • Affordable tuition fees compared to Western countries • Scholarships and financial aid widely available for international students • Strong focus on innovation, science, and engineering disciplines • Vibrant cultural experience in a safe, dynamic environment • Strong government support for international students.

  • How does the education system function in South Korea?

    • Higher education includes universities, technical colleges, and language institutes • Degree programs include undergraduate, graduate, and doctoral levels with research emphasis • Many universities offer English-taught programs especially at postgraduate level • Academic curriculum combines theoretical knowledge with practical skills.

  • What is the climate like in South Korea?

    • Four distinct seasons: cold, snowy winters and hot, humid summers • Spring and autumn are mild and pleasant, ideal for outdoor activities • Students should prepare appropriate clothing for seasonal changes including winter gear.

  • Stay Back & Future Opportunities

    • International students can apply for student work permits during their studies • Post-study work visa opportunities available for graduates to seek employment in Korea • Strong job market in technology, manufacturing, and services sectors • IIEC provides guidance on internships, work permits, and long-term career planning

  • What is the climate like in South Korea Currency?

    • Official currency is the South Korean Won (KRW) • Familiarity with exchange rates and budgeting in KRW is important for managing living expenses • As of October 2025, 1 Indian Rupee (INR) is approximately 15.3 South Korean Won (KRW).

  • How would you describe the Culture of South Korea?

    • Rich cultural heritage blending traditional and modern influences • Focus on respect, community, and academic excellence • Active student life with clubs, festivals, and cultural events • Welcoming attitude towards international students with various support networks

  • How is the Survivability in South Korea?

    • High standard of safety and public health services • Modern infrastructure with quality healthcare accessible to students • Universities provide counseling, orientation, and assistance programs • Students tend to find a supportive community with peers and local residents
